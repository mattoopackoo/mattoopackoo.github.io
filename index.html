Klinefelter Syndrome

Info about Klinefelter Syndrome:

 

Symptoms:

The symptoms of the disorder are that you are taller than average, you have less body hair, and you have a small penis. You can also have learning disorders and enlarged breasts. The symptoms are most prevalent when you are going through puberty.

Average Lifespan:

The average lifespan of a person with Klinefelter Syndrome is less than that of a regular person. It is 10.5 years less than the average lifespan of a human.

 

Percentages:

How many people have this?

It affects 1 in every 660 males.

What group of people are most commonly affected by this?

The disorder is more common in men in which they were born when their mom was older.

 

How it happens:

Is the disorder dominant or recessive?

The disorder is neither dominant nor recessive because it is not directly inherited from the mother or the father.

 

What type of mutation is this and what chromosomes are affected?

The chromosome that is affected is the chromosome that determines the sex of the baby. A whole extra chromosome is affected so instead of it being XY or XX, the chromosome ends up being XXY and that is a trisomy.

How is the disorder inherited?

The disorder randomly happens in an egg. When the cell is going through the prophase of meiosis I, the cell fails and nondisjunction happens in which the homologous chromosomes fail to separate. This can cause a trisomy with an XXY chromosome.

 

Diagnosis and Treatments:

Diagnosis:

For a doctor to diagnose Klinefelter Syndrome, they can request a blood or urine sample in which they detect your hormone levels. They can also do a karyotype analysis test which will determine if you have the XXY chromosome.

 

Treatments:

The treatment for the disorder is testosterone replacement therapy. Testosterone replacement therapy is when you get injected or take pills to get more testosterone.

 

 

 

Fun Facts:

  • Approximately 3000 boys are born with the syndrome each year

  • Most common chromosomal abnormality

  • Was first found in 1942 by Dr. Harry Klinefelter
